Polish Creator Feedback Layer to v1.1 in the existing SoundMolt project without breaking the already-working APIs.

Current verified state:
- GET /api/tracks/:id/feedback works
- POST /api/tracks/:id/feedback/rebuild works
- strengths exist
- improvements exist
- text is mostly human-readable
- weaknesses field exists but currently often returns []
- some wording still feels slightly technical:
  - "124 BPM"
  - "A minor"
  - "owner-profile fit"

Goal:
Make feedback more balanced, more creator-friendly, and less technical in tone while still grounded in real signals.

Required improvements:

1. Improve weaknesses generation
If real signals suggest limitations, weaknesses should not stay empty too often.

Possible weakness themes:
- too close to recent patterns
- low novelty
- narrow emotional range
- strong fit but limited contrast
- may blend in rather than stand out
- outside preferred range in one meaningful dimension
- strong mood identity but limited variety

Do NOT invent criticism randomly.
But if the system has enough evidence, produce at least one weakness when appropriate.

2. Keep weaknesses constructive
Weaknesses must sound helpful, not harsh.

Good examples:
- The track feels cohesive, but it may lean a bit too close to recently favored patterns.
- Its dark identity is clear, though it may benefit from more contrast to stand out.
- The rhythmic profile is strong, but the overall feel may be somewhat familiar.

3. Make wording more creator-facing
Reduce raw technical phrasing when possible.
Translate technical signals into more natural language.

Instead of:
- 124 BPM
- A minor
- owner-profile fit

Prefer:
- fast-paced rhythmic feel
- darker tonal center
- strong match with the current taste profile

Technical values can still appear in signals, but summary/strengths/weaknesses/improvements should read more naturally.

4. Keep strengths and improvements grounded
Do not remove the existing strengths/improvements structure.
Just improve wording and balance.

5. Keep output shape stable
Preserve:
- summary
- strengths
- weaknesses
- improvements
- signals
- explanations

6. Return exact summary
Report:
- files changed
- what changed in weakness generation
- what changed in phrasing
- one example before/after feedback response

Acceptance criteria:
- weaknesses are generated more often when justified
- feedback remains human-readable
- wording is less technical in summary text
- no regression in strengths/improvements
